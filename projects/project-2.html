Exploring Arduino
The Robotics for Space Exploration (RSX) Hackathon was our very first hackathon. Our team of 4 consisted of Kristin Mark, Maria Betty, Preetinder Signh, and myself. We thought that this was one of the best hackathons as a start since Maria and I were interested in coding and Kristin and Preet were interested in the electrical hardware components. The main objective of this hackathon was to build a robot that tracked a black line on the ground through various twists and turns. Our team, named the “Roblox Warriors” placed third place overall out of 30 teams!!! It was a major success, and a lot of fun being able to learn and work with my best friends.
I think that this hackathon was another prime example of my learning style preference. Going into this hackathon, there were 4 lectures online that were youtube videos but I felt as though I knew nothing and was pretty scared of letting my friends down. However, as the hackathon progressed, Maria and I started getting better at figuring out how to code the bang bang algorithm that ultimately made it to the end of the course! I really feel like this hackathon was when I truly discovered how much faster I could learn things when I had a hands-on learning experience, and it has changed my approach on new projects ever since. I am no longer reluctant to start new unknown projects and dive into a different learning process.
Since our hackathon was only about 8 hours, I do not believe that we gained a lot of experience and knowledge on Arduino and robotics. However, this hackathon has taught me that having hands-on experience while learning new materials drastically changes the learning experience and process. I truly wish that I can one day find or develop methods to learn tough subjects such as calculus and linear algebra with a more hands on approach. This hackathon has changed my preferred learning style and I will be forever grateful to RSX for providing me with this opportunity and experience.
