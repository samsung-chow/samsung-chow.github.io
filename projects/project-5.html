The portable Coin Sorting Solution
During Praxis one, our team addressed the issue of a lack in current portable coin sorting solutions. One of the main reasons for today's transition into a cashless world is because of the inefficiency and inconvenience of change and their storage. In order to address that, our team designed the coin sorting shaker machine, which uses the vibrations of walking to automatically sort your coins. Shown on the left is a prototype that was brought to Alpha (it was rotated to fit onto the screen). This prototype was built out of a used pringles can and 3d printed layers for the sorting mechanism.
The sorting mechanism was based off of the lego head sorting machine. With each layer having smaller holes, larger currency is filtered at the top and smaller currencies such as dimes and pennies would fall to the bottom. Each layer was 3d printed to the exact size of each coin such that each layer would sort one type of coin specifically. Additionally, this design featured an automatic sliding door for coin insertion and an automatic sliding door for coin removal as well. The goal of this design was to eliminate the inconvenience of everyday coin usage.
